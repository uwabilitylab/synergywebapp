Muscle Synergies

Muscle synergies are used to quantify a person's motor control. The more synergies that are needed to accurately represent their motor control, the more complex the motor control. Muscles synergies are used to represent both upper and lower limb motor control.

Motivation

Cerebral Palsy and Stroke

Every brain injury with cerebral palsy and stroke is unique. This results in highly individual gait impairments and thus requires patient-specific metrics to quantify motor control. Quantifying motor control analytically is less common in practice, but could lend insight into individualized treaments.

Motivation

With cerebral palsy, every brain injury is unique. Because of this, patient-specific metrics at needed to quantify impairments.

Motivation

Muscle synergies are calculated through non-negative matrix factorization (NNMF) of electromyography (EMG) signals.

Motivation

The more synergies needed to accurately represent the EMG patterns, the more complex the gait and motor control.
